Welcome to our visualization tool for TikTok usage among French-speaking media

About us

In this website, we offer 5 different data visualizations centered around the habits of 40 different French-speaking media outlets on TikTok. We also compare viewer engagement for each of the 21,972 videos in our dataset, and attempt to demonstrate correlations of this engagement to characterstics such as the hashtags used, the video length, the songs used and so on. In each of our visualizations, we measure viewer engagement seperately through 4 metrics: views, likes, comments and shares. Whether you're an industry professional, a journalist, a researcher, or simply someone interested in the world of French media on TikTok, our website will provide you interesting and relevant information. This website was made in the context of the Data Visualization course (INF8808E) at Polytechnique Montréal. The original data was provided by Jean-Hugues Roy, from UQAM.

This website was created by Maximus Shymko, Zachary Thibodeau, Mohammed Aymen Chebbi, Thierry Ndimis, Augustin Lompo, Murhad Masabbir, Théo Reibell and Yannick Désiré

Explore the data

As stated earlier, we have created 5 different data visualizations, each in their own tab:

  • Media Outlets:

    This visualization shows the evolution of viewer engagement for different media outlets over time.

  • Hashtags:

    This visualization shows the top 10 hashtags that generate the most viewer engagement.

  • Video Length:

    This visualization shows which video lengths generate the most and the least amount of viewer engagement.

  • Songs:

    This visualization shows which songs generate the most and the least amount of viewer engagement.

  • Posting and Engagement:

    This visualization shows the posting habits and viewer engagement for videos during different time slots throughout the week.

Have fun exploring the data!
